<h4><strong>1923-1942</strong></h4>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>1923: The school began as a small attap house known as Chiang Teck School
and was founded by Cai Ke Xie (Chua Keh Hai) and his comrades, who donated
generously to sponsor the school.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>1924: On 21 January, a temporary campus was established at Kampong Bahru
Road, receiving 30 to 40 students per class.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>1925: A new campus was built at 64 Silat Road.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>1926: Mr Chua Eng Moh, the school’s first Principal retired in October.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>1927: Mr Loo Kian Chiang took over as Principal. He kept school fees low
to help the poor families in the area. School fees subsidies also were
provided.</p>
</li>
</ul>
